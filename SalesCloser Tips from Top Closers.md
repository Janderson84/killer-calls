# Tips from SalesCloser's Best Closers
## What the data says. What you can use today.

---

## James Anderson (2025)
**76 won deals · $348K · $4.6K avg · 4-day median close**

James was SalesCloser's top closer in 2025. We analyzed 53 of his actual demo transcripts.

### 1. Build rapport like you actually like the person
James uses casual, warm language 2.5x more than he probes for pain. "Appreciate it, man." "I think you're gonna crush." He makes prospects feel like peers, not targets.

**Try this:** Find your version of warm. It doesn't have to be "buddy" — be yourself. Just be human, not corporate.

### 2. Close five times before you get to the close
James averages 5.7 close signals per call — one every 7 minutes. "I'll send you the statement of work." "Let's get you set up with onboarding." "Calendar invite coming your way." By the time he formally asks, the prospect has already said yes three times.

**Try this:** Count your close bridges next demo. If you have fewer than 5 in a 30-minute call, you're not closing enough.

### 3. Let the demo do the discovery work
James asks only 2-3 direct discovery questions per call. He learns about the prospect by showing the product and watching their reactions. Each feature becomes a conversation starter.

**Try this:** Instead of "Tell me about your business," show them the voice agent and ask "How would this handle your inbound calls?" Demo-as-discovery.

### 4. Read the room — adapt your talk ratio
James averages 64% talk share, but ranges from 27% to 94%. Quiet prospect? He fills the space. Talkative prospect? He listens and lets them sell themselves.

**Try this:** If the prospect has been quiet for 2 minutes, you're talking too much. If they've been talking for 5, shut up and take notes — they're already sold.

### 5. Schedule the followup before you hang up
James's fastest close was 8 minutes (Ramon Cerrato, $3K). How? The followup was already on the calendar. No "I'll send you a proposal and we'll go from there." No limbo.

**Try this:** End every demo with a specific next meeting. "Thursday 10am for onboarding?" Not "What works for you?"

## Marc Bowring (2025)
**100 won deals · $528K · $5.3K avg · 5-7 demos/day**

Marc was the volume king. Highest total revenue. Runway of deals kept his pipeline full.

### 1. Volume beats everything
Marc ran 5-7 demos per day. More at-bats = more wins. He didn't overthink any single deal because there was always another one coming.

**Try this:** Aim for 3+ demos per day minimum. If your calendar doesn't have 15 demos this week, your pipe isn't full enough.

### 2. Prepare like you've already met them
Marc came to every demo with the prospect's company open, their LinkedIn reviewed, and a specific use case in mind before they said a word. Preparation breeds confidence.

**Try this:** 5 minutes of prep per demo. Know their company, their role, and one specific thing you'll show them that's relevant.

### 3. Long game, bigger deals
Marc's deals were larger ($5.3K avg) but took longer. He played the relationship over multiple calls, building trust before asking for commitment. Not everyone is a one-call close — and that's fine if the deal size justifies it.

**Try this:** If someone needs time, don't panic. Set a specific followup with a reason ("I'll bring you case studies from similar agencies") and keep the deal warm.

### 4. Systematic, not scattered
Marc had a process. Same prep every time. Same followup sequence. Same close pattern. He didn't wing it — he executed a system.

**Try this:** Write down your demo flow and follow it. Not a script — a structure. Intro → Show → Connect → Close → Schedule.

## Bob Oshidary (2025)
**100 won deals · $286K · $2.9K avg · one-call close**

Bob was the closer's closer. Demo = close. No second call. No "thinking about it."

### 1. Assume the close from minute one
Bob's default state is "we're doing this." His language reflects it: "When you're onboarded..." not "If you decide to move forward..."

**Try this:** Listen to your next demo recording. Count how many times you say "if" vs "when." Flip every "if" to "when."

### 2. Price anchor high, then deliver value
Bob leads with pricing, not hides from it. He anchors the conversation at the full price, then shows value that makes it feel cheap.

**Try this:** Price comes in the first 15 minutes. "Our Silver plan is $1,250/mo. Here's what that gets you." Not at the end as a surprise.

### 3. Trial closes test the water
Bob uses trial closes throughout: "Does this sound like it would work for your team?" "Can you see this handling your inbound?" Each "yes" is a micro-commitment that builds toward the real close.

**Try this:** After every major feature demo, ask a trial close question. Three "yeses" before the real ask.

### 4. Documents on the call
Bob sends the SOW, the payment link, the onboarding calendar invite — all while still on the call. No "I'll email that over." The deal closes while the energy is high.

**Try this:** Have your contract link and payment link open in a browser tab before the demo starts. Send it before the call ends.

## The Common Thread

| | James | Marc | Bob |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Style** | Rapport-first | Systematic | Assumptive |
| **Close** | 5.7 per call, ambient | Progressive, multi-touch | One-call, price-anchored |
| **Cadence** | Demo → close in 4 days | 5-7 demos/day | Demo = close |
| **Deal size** | $4.6K avg | $5.3K avg | $2.9K avg |
| **Talk ratio** | 64% (adaptive) | More balanced | 75-80% (dominant) |

**All three do one thing the same: they close on every call.** None of them end a demo with "I'll send you a proposal and we'll go from there."

## Your Playbook

1. **Steal James's rapport.** Be a human. Warm wins.
2. **Steal Marc's volume.** 3+ demos a day. More at-bats.
3. **Steal Bob's certainty.** "When you're live" not "if you decide."
4. **Close 5+ times per call.** Not pushy — natural. Bridge every feature to "here's how you get started."
5. **Schedule the next step on the call.** No limbo. No "I'll circle back."
