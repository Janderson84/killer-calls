## Phase 2 — DISCOVERY (10-15 minutes)

**"We are not selling yet." This phase is about understanding their world deeply enough to prescribe the right solution. The QUICK framework handles discovery AND qualification in a single flow — no separate framework needed.**

### Q — Qualify Current State *(3-4 min)*

*Understand their current world before diagnosing problems.*

1. Walk me through your current sales/client management process end to end
2. What does your team's typical call flow look like? (volume, length, steps)
3. What qualifying questions are you asking leads today?
4. What's the goal action you're driving toward? (appt booking, call booking, closed sale)
5. What tools or automation have you tried before?
   - *(Social proof opportunity: "A lot of our clients come in having tried X...")*

### U — Uncover & Amplify Pain *(3-4 min)*

*Dig into the real problem. Reciprocate and validate as they share. This is the most important step — missed 80% of the time.*

1. What's breaking in that process right now? What's not working the way you need it to?
2. Why is that a problem for you specifically? (revenue impact, time, missed opportunities)
3. How long has this been going on?
4. What have you tried to fix it so far, and why didn't it stick?
5. **Amplify the pain:** "What does that cost you per month right now?" / "If nothing changes in 6 months, what does that look like for your business?"

**Coaching note: The answers here become your ammunition in the close. Write down exact numbers and phrases they use. You'll mirror these back when closing.**

### I — Identify Budget & Timeline *(2-3 min)*

*Surface budget expectations and buying timeline. These two are tied together — urgency without budget is a wish, budget without urgency is a maybe.*

1. Have you allocated budget toward solving this? What range are you working with?
2. When do you need this solved by? What's driving that date?
3. Is there a launch, season, or growth target that makes this urgent?
4. What happens if you miss that window?

### C — Confirm Decision Process *(2-3 min)*

*Map the evaluation criteria and who needs to weigh in.*

1. If you could design the perfect solution, what would it do?
2. Who else needs to weigh in on this decision? *(You might already know this from Phase 1.2)*
3. What would need to be true for you to move forward?

**QUICK Checkpoint — By now you should have a clear picture of:**

- **Q — Qualify:** Their current setup, tools, team, call volume
- **U — Uncover & Amplify:** The specific pain, what it's costing them, what future state they want
- **I — Identify Budget:** Budget range surfaced, timeline and urgency anchored
- **C — Confirm Decision:** Who makes the call and what "great" looks like

*If any of these are unclear, circle back before moving to the demo.*

### K — Keep Moving *(1 min)*

*Don't get stuck in discovery. Summarize and transition efficiently.*

1. "So just to make sure I've got this right — you're currently doing [Q], which is causing [U], you're looking to solve it by [I timeline], and the decision comes down to [C]. That sound about right?"

## Phase 5 — CLOSE: 123 Closing

**The 123 Closing approach: (1) Summarize value, (2) Handle concerns, (3) Ask for the business. Pick the method that fits the moment.**

---

### Step 1 — Summarize Value *(Always do this first)*

*Mirror their own words back to them. Connect discovery pain points → demo solution → business outcomes.*

**Soft close script:**

"From where I'm sitting this sounds like a perfect fit. So this makes a lot of sense for you right now, eh? You can see yourselves using this? Sounds like this would be a great fit for you right now."

**Tie back to their specific pain points using their language:**
- "You mentioned [Pain from U step] was costing you [Impact number from U step]..."
- "We showed you how the agent handles [specific use case from demo], which directly solves that..."
- "And with your [timeline from I step] coming up, getting this live by [date] means you'll be ready."

---

### Step 2 — Handle Concerns *(Before you ask for the close)*

"Was there anything else you were hoping to see?"

- Address any remaining questions or hesitations
- If objections arise, use the **ECIR framework:**
  - **E**mpathize → **C**larify → **I**solate → **R**espond

**Common concerns and how to handle them:**
- "I need to talk to my team" → Reference team question from Phase 1.2 / 5a; offer a follow-up call with decision maker
- "I need to think about it" → Revisit Impact: "You mentioned this is costing you X per month. Every month you wait is another X lost."
- "The price is too high" → Reframe around ROI using their numbers from the U step

---

### Step 3 — Ask for the Business *(Choose your closing method)*

**Pick the technique that fits the conversation. Read the room and choose one. If your first attempt stalls, pivot to another.**

#### A: The Assumptive Close
**Best when: Prospect is positive, engaged, no major objections.**

Act as though the decision is already made and move straight into logistics.

- "Great — let me get the SOW over to you so we can lock in your training slot. Do you have 30 minutes tomorrow between X-X or [next day] at X?"
- "Cool, I'll send over the payment link now so we can reserve your spot."

#### B: The Alternative Close
**Best when: Prospect is interested but hasn't committed. Give them a choice between two "yes" options.**

- "Would you want to start with the Silver package to cover inbound and outbound, or does the Basic make more sense to get your feet wet first?"
- "Would it work better to kick off this week or start fresh on Monday?"

#### C: The Urgency Close
**Best when: There's a real deadline or time pressure from discovery (I step).**

Tie back to their timeline and the cost of waiting.

- "You mentioned you need this live before [deadline]. If we get started today, we can have your agent trained and running by [date]. Every week we wait pushes that back."
- "We're onboarding a batch of new clients this week, and I can get your training call in the queue if we lock this in today."

#### D: The Summary Close
**Best when: Multiple pain points addressed. Stack the value before the ask.**

Walk through everything they're getting, point by point:

- "So just to recap — you're getting an AI agent that handles [use case], solves [pain from U step], saves you roughly [impact number] per month, and you'll be live before [deadline from I step]. The [package] at $X/mo covers all of that. Want me to send over the details?"

#### E: The Trial Close / Temperature Check
**Best when: You're not sure where they stand and need to gauge before the hard close.**

Ask a low-pressure question to test commitment:

- "On a scale of 1-10, how confident are you that this solves the problem we talked about?"
- "If we could get the pricing to work, is this something you'd want to move on this week?"
- Use their answer to decide whether to go for A/B/C/D or circle back to Step 2

#### F: The Puppy Dog Close
**Best when: Prospect likes it but is hesitant. Let the product sell itself.**

- "Tell you what — let's get your agent set up, get you into a training call, and you can see it working with your real use case. Once you see the calls coming in, I think you'll see exactly what I mean."

**After the close attempt:** If they say yes, move to Steps 9-13 below. If they stall, return to Step 2 (Handle Concerns) and try a different method.
