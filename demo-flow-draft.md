## Framework Reference (Know These Cold)

| Framework | Phase | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **BANT** | Pre-Demo / Discovery | Qualifies whether this is a real opportunity |
| **SPICED** | Discovery | Maps the full picture of their world and buying motivation |
| **SVC** | Presentation → Close | Structures the transition from demo to committed next step |
| **ECIR** | Objections | Handles pushback without caving |

**BANT** = Budget · Authority · Need · Timeline
**SPICED** = Situation · Pain · Impact · Critical Event · Decision
**SVC** = Summary · Value · Close
**ECIR** = Empathize · Clarify · Isolate · Respond

## Phase 1 — Intro & Pre-Qualification

### 1. Who We Are
*"World's first conversational AI for phone and Zoom calls."*
- If they struggle to visualize it → show the Morgan GIF on SalesCloser.ai homepage.

### 2. Authority Check *(BANT — A)*
> "Before we dive in — is there anyone else on your team who typically weighs in on automation or sales tools? Are they available to jump on right now?"

**Why this matters:** Getting the decision-maker on the call now is 10x easier than scheduling them later. If they can't join → note it, and build the "bring them in" step into your close.

- If yes → bring them on
- If no → *"No worries, we'll circle back at the end. I want to make sure you have everything you need to loop them in."*

### 3. Agenda *(Earn buy-in — don't just announce it)*
> "Here's how I'd like to use our time today. First, I want to understand your business, your current process, and what's not working. Then I'll show you exactly how we'd build a SalesCloser to solve that — not a generic demo, one built around your situation. And then we'll figure out if and how it makes sense to move forward. Does that work for you?"

**Checkpoint:** Wait for a yes. This is your first micro-close.

## Phase 2 — Discovery
> ⚠️ **We are not selling yet.** Your job here is to listen, not pitch. The more they talk, the better your demo will be.

Work through **SPICED** and **BANT** together — they're not separate checklists, they're a conversation. The SPICED questions drive the narrative; BANT fills in the qualification gaps.

---

### S — Situation *(What does their world look like right now?)*

> "Walk me through your current process — how do leads come in, what happens next, and where does it usually break down?"

Follow-ups:
- *"What does your team's typical call volume look like — how many calls a day?"*
- *"What's the goal action you're trying to drive? Booked appointment, closed sale, something else?"*
- *"What tools or automation have you tried before?"* ← Social proof hook: *"A lot of our clients come in having tried X..."*

**BANT — Budget signal:** Listen for indicators of team size, current spend, and whether they've paid for tools before. Don't ask directly yet.

---

### P — Pain *(What's actually broken?)*

> "What's not working the way you need it to in that process?"

Follow-ups:
- *"Why is that a problem for you specifically — what's it costing you?"*
- *"How long has this been going on?"*
- *"What have you tried to fix it, and why didn't it stick?"*

**Coaching note:** Reciprocate and validate as they share. Don't rush past pain — this is where trust is built and where your demo gets its narrative.

---

### I — Impact *(Quantify the cost of doing nothing)*
> ⚠️ **This is the most commonly skipped step. Don't skip it.**

> "What is this costing you right now — in deals, hours, or dollars?"

Follow-ups:
- *"If nothing changes in the next 6 months, what does that look like for the business?"*
- *"How is this affecting your team beyond just the revenue number?"*

**Goal:** Get a number. Even a rough one. *"So you're probably losing 3–4 deals a month to missed follow-up?"* Anchor this — you'll use it in your SVC.

---

### C — Critical Event *(What creates urgency?)*

> "When do you need this solved by? Is there something driving that timeline?"

Follow-ups:
- *"Is there a launch, a season, or a growth target that makes this urgent?"*
- *"What happens if you miss that window?"*

**BANT — Timeline:** This is your T. If they can't name a deadline, create one: *"Most clients take 2–3 weeks to get fully onboarded. If you want this live by [date], we'd need to move this week."*

---

### E — Decision Process *(Who decides and how?)*

> "If you could design the perfect solution, what would it do?"

Follow-ups:
- *"Who else needs to weigh in on this decision?"* ← **BANT — Authority** (second pass)
- *"What would need to be true for you to move forward?"*
- *"Have you looked at anything else, or is this your first conversation about solving this?"*

**BANT — Budget (direct ask):** Once you understand their situation, ask directly:
> *"Just so I can point you to the right package — do you have a budget range in mind for solving this, or is that something we'd figure out together?"*

---

### Discovery Recap *(Before moving to demo)*

Reflect back what you heard — don't read from notes, synthesize it:

> *"So let me make sure I have this right. You're [situation], and the main thing that's not working is [pain]. That's costing you roughly [impact]. You've got [timeline] to get this sorted, and [decision process]. Does that sound right?"*

**This is your handoff.** If they confirm → you now have permission to show them exactly how you'd solve it.

Return to authority check:
> *"And [decision-maker's name] — what would they need to see to get comfortable with a solution like this? Features, price, ROI?"*
> *"When's your next standing meeting with them?"* ← Soft-schedule the follow-up now if they're not on the call.

## Phase 3 — Demo

> ⚠️ **MONOLOGUES KILL SALES.** No unbroken talking stretch longer than 90 seconds. Check in constantly.

### Dashboard & Setup Walk-through
- Share screen
- Walk through Creating an Agent with a Prompt
- **Company Info** — customize for their brand
- **Agent Info** — position every setting as solving the specific pain they named
- Twilio account for outbound (if relevant)
- *"You can make it sound like whatever you want — I'll use the default settings for this demo."*
- **Schedule Meetings** / 3 Stage Prompt Builder

*⏱ Target: 5 minutes to this point*

---

### Show the Agent Live

Paint the picture before you dial:
> *"Imagine this is what's picking up your calls right now. Your team is with clients — this handles it."*
> *"Instead of voicemail, they get this."*

Make the call → let them hear it.

> *"Isn't that something? Imagine having that running 24/7. Let me show you what gets captured in the dashboard..."*

---

### Demo Recap *(Tie-down before SVC)*

> *"Before we move on — any questions? Anything you wanted to see more of?"*

Recap their specific pain points and name exactly how the agent addresses them. This is not a feature recap — it's a pain→solution map.

## Phase 4 — SVC (Summary · Value · Close)

This is the most structured part of the call. Follow the sequence.

---

### S — Summary
Compress everything into 2–3 sentences:

> *"So here's where we are. You told me [pain point 1] and [pain point 2] are costing you roughly [impact]. We built an agent around [their use case] — you just heard it. Based on what you've told me, this is a strong fit."*

---

### V — Value
Tie the product back to their specific ROI:

> *"At [package price], if this books even [X] additional calls per month — or prevents [Y] missed follow-ups — it pays for itself. You told me a single [deal/client] is worth [amount]. This thing pays for itself with one win."*

Don't just state the price. Show the math using *their* numbers.

---

### C — Close
Lead with confidence, not a question:

> *"From where I'm sitting, this is a clear fit. I want to get you set up. If we can put together a package that works for your budget, can I get 30 minutes on your calendar this week for the training call?"*

If they agree → move directly to pricing. Don't re-open discovery.

Soft close alternatives:
- *"Does this make sense for where you are right now?"*
- *"Can you see yourselves using this?"*
- *"If the numbers work, is there any reason we wouldn't move forward today?"*

## Phase 5 — Pricing

### Packages

| Package | Price | Talk Time | Outbound |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | $750/mo | 30 hrs / 960 min | ❌ |
| Silver | $1,250/mo | 60 hrs / 4,800 min | ✅ |
| Gold | $2,000/mo | 200 hrs / 12,000 min | ✅ |

**Rule:** Use their use case to pick the right package. Focus on outcomes, not talk time.

**How to present:**
1. Recap features and tie each one back to their pain points
2. Name the package and price — then **go silent**
3. Let them respond. Don't fill the silence.
4. If they hesitate → ECIR

## Phase 6 — Objection Handling (ECIR)

Every objection gets the full sequence. Don't skip steps.

**E — Empathize:** *"That makes total sense..."* / *"I hear you..."*
**C — Clarify:** *"Can I ask — what specifically is the concern?"*
**I — Isolate:** *"If we can work through [specific concern], is there anything else that would get in the way?"*
**R — Respond:** Answer the real objection directly. No deflecting. No pre-emptive discount.

> ⚠️ **Never discount before you've completed ECIR.** Giving ground before you understand the objection trains prospects to push harder.

## Phase 7 — Hard Close & Next Steps

> *"Great — I'll send over the payment link and reserve your training call time slot."*

Drop the link in chat:
> *"Just dropped that in for you. Do you have 30 minutes tomorrow between [time] or [next day] at [time]?"*

Send SOW:
> *"Sending over the details now — if you open that link I can answer any questions as you go."*

Send Payment Link:
> *"And to lock in your training slot, I've included your payment link — first payment secures the team for your onboarding call."*

## Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

```
INTRO
  → Who we are → Authority check (BANT-A) → Agenda buy-in

DISCOVERY (SPICED + BANT)
  → S: Current process, volume, tools
  → P: What's broken and why it matters
  → I: Quantify the cost — get a number ⚠️
  → C: Timeline and urgency (BANT-T)
  → E: Decision process + ideal solution (BANT-A revisited)
  → Budget ask (BANT-B): "What range are you working with?"

RECAP → Confirm understanding → Authority loop-in

DEMO
  → Dashboard → Agent build → Live call
  → Tie-downs throughout — no monologues >90s

SVC
  → S: Compress the story
  → V: ROI math using their numbers
  → C: Ask for the business

PRICING → One package → Silence → ECIR if needed

CLOSE → Payment link → Training call booked
```
